Getting Started

How to Play

1

Know the goal

Arrange the numbers 1 through 9 in a 3x3 grid so each row increases left to right and each column increases top to bottom. The solved state is 1–2–3 on top, 4–5–6 in the middle, 7–8–9 on the bottom.

2

Make a hook move

Tap any cell to activate a hook. The hook spans all cells directly above the tapped cell and all cells to its right, including the tapped cell. Every number shifts forward one step — the last wraps around to the first position in the hook.

3

Use hints wisely

Stuck? The hint button previews the next optimal move. Hints cost extra moves on your final score, so use them only when needed.

4

Beat the optimal

When you solve a puzzle, your move count is compared to the known minimum. The closer you are to optimal, the better your score.

Modes

Game Modes

Standard
🟢

Standard Mode

Tap any cell freely for as long as you need. Great for learning the puzzle and building intuition for hook moves.

Constrained
🔴

Constrained Mode

Once you move away from a cell, it locks permanently and turns red. Every tap counts — plan your sequence before you commit.

Frequently Asked Questions

FAQ

What exactly is a hook move?
When you tap a cell, the hook is formed by all cells in that column above the tapped cell, plus all cells in that row to the right, including the tapped cell itself. Every number in the hook shifts forward by one position, and the last number wraps back to the first cell in the hook.
Why did a cell turn red?
You are in Constrained Mode. When you tap a cell and then move to a different cell, the first cell locks and turns red. Locked cells cannot be tapped again. The top-right cell (position 9) is always exempt from locking.
What does the difficulty slider do?
The slider on the main menu selects a difficulty range. Lower difficulty puzzles require fewer moves to reach the solution. The hardest setting pulls from puzzles with the longest known optimal solution lengths.
Can I resume a game I left mid-way?
Yes. If you exit using the in-game settings button, your progress is saved automatically. Open Settings and tap Resume to continue where you left off. Choosing Back to Home from Settings will clear your saved game.
What is the optimal move count?
Each puzzle has a pre-computed minimum number of moves required to reach the solution. Your final move count is compared to this on the win screen.
How do I mute the game?
Tap the sound button in the Settings menu. Your preference is saved and persists across sessions.
About

The Math Behind Hook It

Hook It is based on the mathematics of standard Young tableaux — arrangements of numbers in a grid where every row increases left to right and every column increases top to bottom. That target state is exactly what you are solving for.

The hook move derives from the promotion operator on tableaux, a classical operation in algebraic combinatorics. Every puzzle in the game comes from a verified solvable configuration with a known optimal solution length.

Hook It was developed by Jeffrey Wang (UCSB CCS Computing), with production managed by Richert Wang. The underlying mathematics are due to Professor Ömer Eğecioğlu at UC Santa Barbara.
