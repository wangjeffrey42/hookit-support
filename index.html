How to Play

The basics

01

The goal

Arrange 1 through 9 so each row increases left to right and each column increases top to bottom. The solution is always 1-2-3, 4-5-6, 7-8-9.

02

Hook moves

Tap any cell. The hook covers all cells above it in the same column, plus all cells to its right in the same row, including itself. Every number shifts forward one step; the last wraps to the front.

03

Score

Your move count is compared to the known optimal for that puzzle. Hints cost extra moves.

Modes

Two ways to play

Standard

Free Play

Tap any cell at any time. No restrictions. Good for learning the mechanics.

Constrained

Locked Cells

Leave a cell and it locks permanently. Every tap is a commitment. Plan ahead.

FAQ

Common questions

Why is a cell red?
You are in Constrained Mode. Red cells are locked — you left them and can no longer tap them. The top-right cell is always exempt from locking.
Can I resume a game?
Yes. Exit using the in-game settings button to save your progress. Open Settings and tap Resume to pick up where you left off. Tapping Back to Home clears the save.
What does the difficulty slider do?
It selects a range of puzzles ordered by optimal solution length. Low difficulty means fewer moves to solve. High difficulty means longer, more complex puzzles.
What is the optimal move count?
Each puzzle has a pre-computed minimum number of moves. Your final count is shown against it on the win screen.
