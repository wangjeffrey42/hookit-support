How to Play

Hook It is a 3x3 sliding puzzle. Your goal is to arrange the numbers 1 through 9 so each row increases left to right and each column increases top to bottom.

Tap any cell to perform a hook move. The hook includes all cells directly above the tapped cell and all cells to its right, including the tapped cell itself. Every number in the hook shifts forward one step, and the last number wraps around to the first position.

Game Modes

Standard: Tap any cell freely until you solve the puzzle.

Constrained: Once you move away from a cell, it locks and cannot be tapped again. Plan your moves carefully.

Difficulty

Use the slider on the main menu to choose a difficulty level. Lower settings give shorter puzzles; higher settings require more moves to solve.

About

Hook It was developed by Jeffrey Wang at UCSB. The puzzle is based on standard Young tableaux theory by Ömer Eğecioğlu. Read the paper.
